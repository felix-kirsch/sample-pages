How long does a roof last in BC?

In BC's wet, variable climate, roofing lifespans are significantly shorter than national averages suggest. Heavy rainfall, UV exposure in the summer months, and freeze-thaw cycles all accelerate wear compared to drier provinces like Alberta or Ontario. BC homeowners who use Ontario lifespan estimates as their planning benchmark consistently get caught short.

The Fraser Valley sits at the extreme wet end of the BC spectrum. Chilliwack and the surrounding communities receive over 1,500mm of annual rainfall — meaning your roof takes more punishment per year than most of the rest of the country.

Signs you need a roof replacement in BC

Some of these signs are visible from the ground. Others require a professional inspection to identify. In the Fraser Valley, where roofs age faster than average, we recommend an annual inspection for any roof over 12 years old — and after any significant storm event regardless of age.

Curling or cupping shingles

Shingles that curl upward at the edges (cupping) or downward in the middle (clawing) have lost their flexibility. This is a direct result of repeated moisture cycling — the shingles expand and contract through BC's wet-dry seasons until they permanently deform. Once shingles are curling, water infiltration is a matter of when, not if.

Granule loss in the gutters

Asphalt shingles lose their protective granule coating over time. Significant granules in your gutters after rainfall means the UV protection layer is compromised. The exposed asphalt beneath will oxidize and crack — accelerating aging and often voiding the manufacturer warranty that requires granule coverage to remain intact.

Moss and algae growth

Common in shaded areas throughout the Fraser Valley. Moss holds moisture against the roof surface for extended periods, accelerating shingle deterioration from below. Regular soft-wash treatment can extend roof life, but heavy moss penetration on an aging roof often signals underlying moisture damage to the decking that won't be fixed by treatment alone.

Interior water stains

Water stains on interior ceilings or attic decking indicate active or past water ingress. In the Fraser Valley, the source is almost always at a penetration point — chimney, skylight, vent pipe — or at a flashing joint, rather than through the middle of a shingle field. A professional inspection will identify the source and determine whether repair or replacement is the right call.

Roofing materials compared for BC conditions

Not all roofing materials perform equally in BC's climate. Here's an honest comparison of the most common options for Fraser Valley homeowners specifically.

Architectural asphalt shingles

The most cost-effective option for most BC homeowners. A quality architectural shingle with a Class 4 impact rating and a properly designed ventilation system performs well in the Fraser Valley's rainfall if maintained annually. Expect 25–30 years with moss treatment and gutter cleaning. Entry-level 3-tab shingles are not recommended for the Fraser Valley climate — the performance gap vs. architectural grade doesn't justify the price difference.

Standing seam metal roofing

The best long-term investment for BC conditions. Concealed fasteners prevent water ingress at fastener points. The metal surface sheds moss naturally without chemical treatment. The material handles freeze-thaw without cracking. The higher upfront cost offsets over time by eliminating 2–3 shingle replacement cycles over the same period. In Chilliwack's rainfall environment, the economics of metal roofing are particularly compelling.

Metal shingles

A middle-ground option that delivers metal durability in a more traditional aesthetic profile. Popular in Sardis, Promontory, and Abbotsford neighbourhoods where curb appeal is a priority. Performance is similar to standing seam with slightly more maintenance requirements at the overlap points over a 40+ year lifespan.

Roof replacement cost in BC

Cost ranges are wide because roofs are wide. Pitch, size, complexity, the number of penetrations (chimneys, skylights, vent pipes), whether tear-off is required, and material specification all affect the final number. Any contractor who quotes by square foot over the phone without seeing your roof is guessing — and guesses tend to become problems at invoice time.

Fraser Valley roof replacement — what's different

The Fraser Valley's combination of high annual rainfall, valley wind events, and freeze-thaw at elevation means roof replacement decisions carry more urgency here than in drier BC regions. A roof that might last 20 years in the Okanagan may fail in 15 in Chilliwack. Factor the local climate into your material choice, your timeline for replacement, and the specification requirements you ask any contractor to meet. A roofer who doesn't mention ventilation, decking condition, and valley metal when quoting your Fraser Valley home hasn't assessed the full scope of the job.

How to choose a roofing contractor in BC

BC requires roofing contractors to hold a valid licence under BC's Homeowner Protection Act. Before signing any contract, confirm the following without exception:

  • The contractor holds a valid BC Contractor Licence — verify at bchousing.org, not just by asking them
  • Full WCB (WorkSafe BC) coverage is active for every crew member who will be on your property
  • The written contract includes: scope of work, materials specification, payment schedule, timeline, and cleanup responsibilities
  • A workmanship warranty is provided in writing, separate from the manufacturer material warranty
  • The contractor carries general liability insurance — ask for the certificate, not just the claim

Red flags that should end your consideration immediately: requests for large deposits before material delivery; refusal to provide a written contract; no address or only a PO box; pressure to sign same-day; inability to provide references from local projects within the last 12 months.

What to expect on installation day

A professional roof installation follows a consistent sequence. Understanding what should happen on your property helps you identify if something is being skipped.

Day one begins with deck protection — tarps covering windows, gardens, AC units, and any outdoor furniture. The existing roofing material is torn off and disposed of. The decking is inspected for soft spots, rot, and moisture damage — any compromised sections are replaced before anything goes over top. Drip edge, ice and water shield at eaves and valleys, and underlayment are installed before a single shingle or panel goes down. On a well-run installation, day one ends with a fully watertight dry-in even if finish material isn't complete.

Finish roofing goes on day two, followed by flashing at all penetrations, ridge cap installation, and full site cleanup. The final walkthrough with the homeowner is where we review the completed work, explain the warranty documentation, and answer any questions before we leave the site.
