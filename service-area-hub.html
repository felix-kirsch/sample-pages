Why Fraser Valley roofs need a local expert

The Fraser Valley has a climate unlike most of BC — and your roofer needs to understand it.

The valley sits bordered by mountains to the north and east, creating a corridor that channels Pacific moisture into some of the highest annual rainfall in the province. The valley floor near the Fraser River deals with relentless moisture-driven moss and algae. Higher-elevation properties in Hope and Ryder Lake face significant snowloads, ice damming, and freeze-thaw cycles that destroy roofs that aren't properly specified for the load.

Beyond the rainfall, the Fraser Valley sees wind events — valley corridor winds from the east that create uplift pressures on roofs built to standard coastal specifications. We've assessed hundreds of roofs after valley wind storms and know exactly what to look for and how to specify installations that hold through them.

A roofer from outside the region doesn't carry this knowledge. We do — and it shows in both our installation standards and our warranty call rate, which is near zero.

Fraser Valley roofing questions

Roof replacement in the Fraser Valley typically ranges from $8,000–$18,000 for asphalt shingles and $18,000–$35,000 for metal roofing on a standard residential home. Costs vary by city, roof size, pitch, and material. We provide free written estimates across all three service areas — Chilliwack, Abbotsford, and Hope.
Metal roofing — particularly standing seam — performs best in the Fraser Valley's high-rainfall environment. It sheds moss naturally, handles freeze-thaw cycles, and lasts 50+ years versus 15–20 for asphalt. For budget-conscious homeowners, a quality architectural shingle with proper ventilation and annual maintenance is a solid alternative.
