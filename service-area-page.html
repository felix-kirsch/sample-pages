Why Chilliwack roofs need extra attention

The Fraser Valley climate is hard on roofing materials — and Chilliwack gets some of the worst of it.

Chilliwack sits in a rain shadow corridor that gets over 1,500mm of annual precipitation. Wet winters mean moss, algae, and pooling water are constant threats — especially on lower-pitch roofs and in shaded areas near tree cover. Sardis and Rosedale properties that back onto forested hillsides are particularly vulnerable to debris accumulation and drainage issues that accelerate early roof failure.

Summer heat swings push asphalt shingles through repeated expansion and contraction cycles, accelerating cracking and granule loss far faster than in drier BC regions. A shingle roof that might last 25 years in the Okanagan often needs replacement in 15 years in Chilliwack — a fact that makes the case for metal roofing stronger here than almost anywhere else in BC.

We've been maintaining, repairing, and replacing Chilliwack roofs through every season for over two decades. Our inspections are built around what actually damages roofs here — not a generic checklist written by someone who's never walked a Promontory acreage in November.
