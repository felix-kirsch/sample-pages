Why metal roofing makes sense in Chilliwack

Chilliwack sits at the foot of the Cascades and receives over 1,500mm of annual rainfall — some of the highest in the Fraser Valley. Traditional asphalt shingles degrade in 15–20 years under these conditions. A metal roof installed by Top Notch Roofing lasts 50+ years, sheds moss naturally, and won't lose granules or blow off in the valley wind events that hit every winter.

Metal roofing also handles the freeze-thaw cycles specific to Chilliwack's elevation range — from valley floor properties near the Fraser River to hillside homes above Promontory and Ryder Lake. Interlocking panels shed ice efficiently, protecting your fascia, gutters, and interior from ice damming — a problem we see every winter in Ryder Lake and on properties above Vedder Mountain.

The economics are compelling too. A standing seam metal roof installed on a Chilliwack home today will still be performing in 2075. Over that same period, an asphalt shingle roof would require replacement two or three times — meaning the metal roof pays for itself and then some, while requiring far less maintenance and generating far fewer warranty calls.
